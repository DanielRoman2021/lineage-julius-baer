Living Wealth Story
A founder who built, then chose what to keep.
Zurich, Switzerland·Born 1976·Married · two children·CHF 180M
Sarah co-founded a Zurich software company in 2009 and spent fourteen years building it into a category leader. In 2023 she sold her majority stake — a liquidity event that reshaped her life faster than her sense of self.
The build · 2009–2023
She bootstrapped the first three years, took a single Series B, and kept a controlling stake throughout. Wealth accumulated quietly inside the business; she drew a modest salary and reinvested almost everything. Risk, for Sarah, always meant concentration — nearly all of her net worth sat in one private holding.
"I never wanted the number to define me. I wanted the freedom to choose what I do with my mornings."
Captured during onboarding · approved by M. Brunner
The exit · 2023
The sale converted a single illiquid position into CHF 180M, of which roughly CHF 62M is liquid today. Her immediate instinct was caution: diversify, protect the family, and avoid lifestyle inflation. The remaining proceeds are being phased into public and private markets over eighteen months.
What matters now
Three threads run through every conversation: time — the wish to step back by 55; family — funding her children's education and a multi-generational legacy; and impact — a structured philanthropic vehicle still taking shape. Freedom is, by her own measure, the area most out of balance.
